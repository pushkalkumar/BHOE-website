Energy to Matter

In the first fractions of a second, the universe was so energetic that energy itself condensed into matter. Einstein's equation — E = mc² — describes this: energy and mass are interconvertible.

E = mc²

A single kilogram of matter contains ~9×10¹⁶ joules — equivalent to a 21-megaton nuclear explosion.

🔥

The Plasma State

For the first 380,000 years, the universe was too hot for atoms to exist. Matter existed as plasma — a state where electrons are stripped from nuclei by thermal energy exceeding ionization energies.

In plasma, kT ≫ IE, so electrons roam freely. The universe was opaque because free electrons scattered all light.

Free protons and electrons in plasma

AP Chemistry Connection — Unit 1: Atomic Structure & Big Idea: Energy

The Recombination event is a direct physical demonstration of ionization energy. As the universe cooled below ~3,000 K, thermal energy (kT) dropped below the ionization energies of hydrogen (~13.6 eV) and helium (~24.6 eV). Electrons could then occupy stable ground-state orbitals — the first electron configurations in cosmic history.

Stars as Nuclear Reactors

How stellar fusion creates every element heavier than lithium — Unit 1: Nuclear Chemistry, Big Idea: Energy & Transformations

The Proton-Proton Chain

In stars like our Sun, hydrogen fuses to helium through the proton-proton chain. The driving force: the strong nuclear force overcomes electrostatic repulsion at temperatures above ~10 million K.

¹H+¹H →²H+ e⁺+ν
Step 1: Deuterium forms
↓

Step 2: Helium-3 forms
↓
³He+³He →⁴He+2¹H
Step 3: Helium-4 + energy released

Net result: 4 protons → 1 helium-4 nucleus + 2 positrons + 2 neutrinos + 2 gamma rays + 26.7 MeV of energy

Binding Energy & the Iron Peak

Each fusion step releases energy because the product nucleus has less mass than its reactants. This "mass defect" converts to energy via E = mc². The binding energy per nucleon peaks at iron-56 — after iron, fusion consumes energy rather than releasing it.

AP Chemistry Connection — Nuclear Chemistry & Big Idea: Energy

Fusion and fission are mirror processes on the binding energy curve. Fusion of light nuclei (below iron) releases energy because the product has higher binding energy per nucleon. Fission of heavy nuclei (above iron) also releases energy for the same reason. Both involve mass defect: ΔE = Δmc².

Scientific Practice 1 — Models & Representations

The Atomic Model Timeline

How evidence forced scientists to revise their understanding of the atom — five models across 123 years

Solid Sphere
Dalton's Atomic Theory1803

John Dalton proposed that all matter is composed of indivisible, indestructible particles called atoms. Each element has atoms of a unique, characteristic mass. Atoms combine in simple, whole-number ratios to form compounds.

Key Evidence

  • Law of Conservation of Mass (Lavoisier, 1774)
  • Law of Definite Proportions (Proust, 1799)
  • Law of Multiple Proportions

Limitation

Could not explain electrical phenomena or why solutions conduct electricity. The atom was treated as truly indivisible — which turned out to be wrong.

Plum Pudding
Thomson's Plum Pudding Model1897

J.J. Thomson's cathode ray experiments demonstrated that all atoms contain negatively charged particles — electrons — far lighter than the lightest atom. He proposed the atom as a diffuse, positively charged sphere with electrons embedded throughout.

Key Evidence

  • Cathode ray tubes deflected identically regardless of electrode material
  • e/m ratio measured: electrons have mass ~1/1837 of hydrogen
  • Atoms are electrically neutral → positive charge must exist somewhere

Limitation

Predicted that alpha particles would pass through with only minimal deflection — dramatically disproved by Rutherford's gold foil experiment.

Nuclear Model
Rutherford's Nuclear Model1911

Geiger and Marsden fired alpha particles at gold foil. Most passed through — but roughly 1 in 8,000 bounced straight back. Rutherford concluded: the atom is mostly empty space, with a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus.

Key Evidence

  • ~99.98% of alpha particles passed through undeflected
  • ~1 in 8,000 deflected at large angles → tiny, dense, positive nucleus
  • Nuclear radius ~10⁻¹⁵ m vs. atomic radius ~10⁻¹⁰ m

Limitation

Classical physics predicts orbiting electrons emit radiation continuously, lose energy, and spiral into the nucleus. Rutherford's model had no explanation for stable atoms.

Planetary/Bohr Model
Bohr's Planetary Model1913

Niels Bohr applied quantum theory to Rutherford's nuclear atom. Electrons occupy specific, allowed energy levels and do not radiate energy while in them. When an electron transitions between levels, it absorbs or emits a photon: ΔE = hν. This explained hydrogen's emission spectrum exactly.

Key Evidence

  • Hydrogen emission spectrum: discrete lines at 656nm, 486nm, 434nm, 410nm
  • Bohr's formula predicted spectral line frequencies for hydrogen with perfect accuracy
  • Explained why atoms don't collapse

Limitation

Only worked for hydrogen (one electron). Failed completely for multi-electron atoms. Could not explain spectral line splitting in magnetic fields.

Quantum Mechanical Model
Quantum Mechanical Model1926

Schrödinger's wave equation describes electrons as probability distributions (wave functions ψ) rather than particles in defined orbits. The square of the wave function |ψ|² gives the probability of finding an electron at a given location. Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle: Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2.

Key Evidence

  • De Broglie (1924): electrons exhibit wave behavior — electron diffraction confirmed
  • Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle: Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2
  • Quantum numbers (n, l, mₗ, mₛ) describe all observable electron properties

Current Understanding

This remains our most accurate model of atomic structure. It successfully explains all atomic phenomena, chemical bonding, and periodic trends.

AP Chemistry Connection — Scientific Practice 1: Models & Representations

This timeline directly demonstrates Scientific Practice 1: how scientific models evolve in response to new experimental evidence. Each revision was forced by data that the previous model could not explain — not by philosophical preference. This iterative process is how science actually works.
